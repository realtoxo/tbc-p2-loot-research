# SSC/TK Weapons and Trinkets Opportunity Cost

This companion document is for loot council decisions where the question is not "is this item BiS?" but "what does the raid lose by giving it here instead of there?"

Source basis:
- Primary LC transcript: Zatar, `TBC Classic Phase 2 Loot Priority [Fixed]`, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xaTf4e4y-mc
- General SSC/TK LC cross-check: Knot, SSC loot discussion, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bNUu6oy2keM
- General SSC/TK LC cross-check: Knot, TK loot discussion, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1HVrxFGDfj4
- Class specialist transcripts in `sources/class-video-transcripts/`
- Wowhead phase 2 class and item pages where a class-specialist video was thin, especially Enhancement Shaman.
- Tier-token companion: [SSC/TK Tier 5 Token Priority and Equip Constraints](ssc-tk-tier-token-priority.md)

## LC Short Version

1. Protect unique role items first: `Serpent Spine Longbow` to Hunter, `Talon of Azshara` to Rogue, `Rod of the Sun King` to Enhance, `Lightfathom Scepter` to a core healer, `Serpent-Coil Braid` to Mage, `Warp-Spring Coil` to Rogue.
2. Treat `Tsunami Talisman` as DST-compensation, not a universal melee trophy. Top practical lane is Feral Cat/Arms, then non-DST Hunter/Fury/Enhance. Rogue and Ret should be out in normal circumstances.
3. Do not let "Hunter melee stat stick" beat real melee weapon progression unless the raid has explicitly decided the Hunter survival utility is worth it.
4. Arcane Mage 2p T5 stays the biggest early Hero-token priority, but once mages have 2p, do not let mage gravity consume every caster weapon/offpiece. Ele, Boomkin, Shadow, and Warlock all have narrow high-impact claims.
5. For healers, the Vashj mace is more about core-player reliability than strict class math. Resto Shaman and Holy Paladin often gain slightly more from replacing `Light's Justice`, but Priest/Druid compensation matters if they are carrying the raid.
6. Future `Warglaives of Azzinoth` planning should be a set-order decision, not a loose weapon roll. Rogue and Fury both use them for the rest of TBC; Rogue is the normal first set unless your Fury is clearly the stronger long-term raid investment.
7. Call out longevity explicitly. A same-week upgrade should not beat a multi-phase weapon, trinket, or later hit/crit/threat swap unless the raid has already covered the long-lived users.

## Longevity and Later Swap Flags

| Item | Long-lived or swap use | LC implication |

| `Rod of the Sun King` | Enhancement Phase 2 BiS and strong Fury offhand path | First `Rod` to Enhance. Later rods can solve Fury offhand if `Talon` is protected for Rogue. |

## Fury Warrior Offhand Ladder

Assumption: normal Phase 2 Fury with `Dragonstrike`/`Dragonmaw` main-hand if Hammersmith. If not Hammersmith, use the main-hand note in the spec table below.

| Offhand | Source | How to treat it for LC |
|---|---|---|
| `Talon of Azshara` | Morogrim | Best Fury offhand in SSC/TK. Rogue still has the first claim because it is their main-hand until Warglaives. Fury should be a real second-lane candidate, not an afterthought. |
| `Rod of the Sun King` | Kael'thas | Strong Fury offhand alternative, especially if `Talon of Azshara` is going Rogue-first for a while. First `Rod` should usually go Enhance; after Enhance, Fury is a serious candidate. Proc is weaker in offhand than main-hand. |
| `Merciless Gladiator's Slicer` / `Merciless Gladiator's Cleaver` | Season 2 PvP | Strong non-raid offhand path. The 2.6 speed avoids the 2.7/2.7 swing-sync issue with `Dragonstrike`, so a PvP-capable Fury can reduce raid loot pressure. |
| `Netherbane` | Al'ar | Good Orc offhand option because axe racial matters. Also a fallback if your raid is heavily protecting `Talon` for Rogue and `Rod` for Enhance. |
| `Spiteblade` | Netherspite, Karazhan | Still a respectable fallback. Many Phase 2 offhands are not enough of an upgrade to justify heavy LC pressure over this. |
| Daggers such as `Heartrazor` / `Fang of Vashj` | Solarian/Vashj | Avoid for Fury DPS. They look good on stats, but dagger normalization hurts Whirlwind/ability damage. These are Rogue/tank-threat items, not Fury loot targets. |
| `Claw of the Phoenix` | Al'ar | Not a Fury target. It is a Hunter stat stick and too fast/awkward for Fury compared with the options above. |

Practical LC read:
- Fury does deserve an offhand plan.
- The cleanest path is `Dragonstrike` MH plus `Talon of Azshara` OH after Rogue.
- If Rogue is getting every `Talon`, push Fury toward `Rod` after Enhance or a Season 2 PvP weapon.
- If the Fury is Orc, `Netherbane` is more defensible than it is for other races.

## Future Warglaive Planning

`Warglaives of Azzinoth` are BiS for both Combat Rogue and Fury Warrior. Do not treat them like ordinary MH/OH drops.

Recommended LC process:

1. Pick a first set recipient before Black Temple.
2. Give both MH and OH to that same recipient as they drop.
3. Pick a second set recipient only after the first set is locked in.

Default first set:
- Combat Rogue first, then Fury Warrior.

When Fury can be first:
- Your Fury is a better and more reliable long-term player than the Rogue.
- Your Rogue roster is unstable, weak, or likely to quit.
- Your raid values the Fury's overall T6/Sunwell contribution and attendance more than the Rogue's larger single-target gain.

Do not use "Fury may get glaives later" as a reason to starve them in SSC/TK. The drop rate is too low and too uncertain. Conversely, do not use "Fury wants glaives" as a reason to give them Rogue-priority `Talon of Azshara`; Talon remains Rogue first in Phase 2.

|---|---|---|---|
| `Serpent-Coil Braid` | Arcane Mage | Any Mage order by performance/attendance | Mage-only in practice. NicB still rates staff/tier timing above hoarding for this because only a few mages can use it. |
| `Warp-Spring Coil` | Rogue | Rogue order by performance/attendance | Rare class trinket that is actually excellent. Do not penalize it just because it is uncontested. |
| `Tsunami Talisman` | Feral Cat/Arms as DST-compensation | Non-DST Hunter/Fury/Enhance | Knot's useful framing: Feral Cat and Arms were commonly shunned on DST, so Tsunami can repair that. Hunters usually prefer DST/Brooch; Enhance ideally gets DST or DMC Crusade; Rogue and Ret should pass. |

| `Darkmoon Card: Crusade` | Ret/Enh/Shadow and other high-uptime users | Bought item, not raid LC | Its existence lowers `Tsunami Talisman` pressure. Ret specialist calls it strong when uptime is maintained and notes Ret auto/Judgement behavior can stack spell damage. |

| `Eye of Magtheridon` | Shadow/Ele if no better | Prior raid item | Shadow specialist prefers DMC Crusade if available; Ele specialist is skeptical because resisted-spell proc is unreliable. |
| `Badge of Tenacity` | Feral Bear/Survival Hunter utility | Bought/world source | Early release weakens Bear's claim on Tsunami and creates a Survival raid-DPS utility option, but it is personal DPS loss for Survival. |

## Spec-by-Spec Notes

| Spec | Weapon focus | Trinket focus | Opportunity-cost note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prot Warrior | `Mallet of the Tides`, `Fang of Vashj` for threat if no dagger Rogue | Tank trinkets by encounter; `Solarian's Sapphire` is niche utility | Do not give caster/tank spell weapons here. Main question is mitigation vs threat set, not raw BiS list. |
| Prot Paladin | `Darkener's Grasp` from first Verdant Sphere; `Fang of the Leviathan` only in threat-heavy guilds | `Scarab of Displacement`/odd tank trinkets are niche | Fang to Prot Paladin is a guild-style decision. In normal guilds, Warlock weapon gain is cleaner. |
| Feral Bear | `Wildfury Greatstaff` | `Badge of Tenacity` weakens Tsunami need | Bear can use Tsunami for threat, but Cat/Arms usually gain more from the scarce trinket. Do not overfeed threat at the cost of T5 mitigation. |
| Feral Cat | Few weapon claims in SSC/TK | `Tsunami Talisman` high if Cat is real DPS | Cat is one of the best Tsunami homes, especially if not receiving DST. If the player mostly tanks, lower it. |
| Rogue | `Talon of Azshara`, later `Arcanite Steam-Pistol` | `Warp-Spring Coil`; keep DST/Brooch | Do not give Tsunami. Do not route Rogue into `Shoulderpads of the Stranger` unless your tier/chest pattern forces it. |
| Fury/Arms Warrior | Fury MH: `Dragonstrike` if Hammersmith; otherwise Human `Rod`, non-Human `Talon of the Phoenix`. Fury OH: `Talon of Azshara` after Rogue. Arms: `Twinblade`. | `Tsunami` if non-DST, especially Arms | Warrior has many claims but few first claims. Reward high performers after class-unique needs are protected. |
| Ret Paladin | `Twinblade` unless staying Lionheart | DST, DMC Crusade, Bloodlust Brooch | Ret does not want Tsunami in the Knot framing. Twinblade is good, but BS Ret with strong Lionheart uptime can wait. |
| Enhancement Shaman | `Rod of the Sun King` plus `Talon of the Phoenix`; 2.7 speed matters | DST/Brooch or DMC Crusade; Tsunami only if DST is unlikely | Enhance is a first-class weapon priority, not a leftover melee. `Shoulderpads of the Stranger`, `Belt of One-Hundred Deaths`, and 4p timing compete with Rogue/Ret plans. |
| BM Hunter | `Serpent Spine Longbow`; `Talon`/`Claw` stat sticks later | DST/Brooch; Tsunami is not a focus | Do not break Beast Lord for random tier singles. Bow is the real P0 item. |
| Survival Hunter | `Serpent Spine Longbow`; `Netherbane` MH + `Claw` OH if investing in agility | `Badge of Tenacity` is a raid-DPS option, personal loss | Avoid AP-only pieces like `Band of the Ranger-General` when playing Survival. Tier legs become more attractive than BM. |
| Arcane Mage | T5 2p first, then `The Nexus Key`/`Gauntlets of the Sun King` | `Serpent-Coil Braid` | Mage receives heavy Hero-token priority. After 2p and one major weapon path, avoid crowding Warlock chest and every caster offpiece. |
| Warlock | `Fang of the Leviathan`, `Fathomstone`, `Wand of the Forgotten Star` | Keeps Icon/Quag; Sextant is not priority | Low Hero-token priority is acceptable only if Warlocks get their offpieces, especially Vashj chest and Solarian wand. PvP spellblade can reduce weapon contention for high-time Warlocks. |
| Elemental Shaman | `The Nexus Key` or Mindblade+`Fathomstone` | Icon plus Lightning Capacitor/Quag; targeted Sextant | Ele T5 set bonuses are weak, so do not also starve Ele from every caster accessory. `Sun King's Talisman` and `Ring of Endless Coils` are major claims. |
| Balance Druid | `The Nexus Key` as a real staff claim | Icon plus `Sextant` in crit/4p build | Boomkin 4p, crit meta, and Sextant interact. It is not just a meme caster trinket if your Boomkin is built around it. |
| Shadow Priest | Usually prior Mindblade/Merciless mace plus badge offhand | Icon; DMC Crusade if available; `Eye of Magtheridon` fallback | `Wand of the Forgotten Star` is a good trade. Shadow can use many lower-contested caster pieces, reducing token/weapon pressure. |
| Holy Paladin | `Lightfathom Scepter`; `Libram of Absolute Truth` is mandatory if it drops | Healing trinkets mostly unchanged | Do not underestimate the libram. For contested loot, Holy Paladin should not outrank every Priest/Druid on cloak/mace just because plate healing gear is awkward. |
| Resto Shaman | `Lightfathom Scepter`, healer rings/boots | Healing trinkets mostly unchanged | Shaman has a slight mace/MP5 argument, but the class also brings critical utility regardless of weapon. Use player reliability to break ties. |
| Holy Priest | `Lightfathom Scepter` eventually, `Luminescent Rod of the Naaru` | Healing trinkets mostly unchanged | Priest has strong tier and spirit cloak/ring claims. If they wait on mace, compensate deliberately. |
| Resto Druid | `Lightfathom Scepter` eventually, spirit staff/offhand options | Healing trinkets mostly unchanged | Druid has strong spirit alternatives and should not be forgotten after Shaman/Paladin mace arguments. |
