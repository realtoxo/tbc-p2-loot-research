# SSC/TK Spec Transcript Synthesis

This document records what the newly captured class-specialist transcripts add to the loot council policy. It is an evidence ledger, not a standalone loot sheet.

Source caveat: these are YouTube caption captures and can contain transcription errors. Use the linked local transcript files for audit, and prefer claims that are corroborated by multiple sources or fit cleanly with Zatar/Knot LC framing.

## Warrior

### Fury Warrior

Source: Ragebtw, `Fury Warrior BIS Phase 2 DEEP DIVE (TBC CLASSIC)`  
Transcript: [txt](sources/class-video-transcripts/ragebtw-fury-warrior-p2-bis.txt), [tsv](sources/class-video-transcripts/ragebtw-fury-warrior-p2-bis.tsv)

| Claim | LC implication |
|---|---|
| Fury Tier 5 set bonuses are weak, and Defender tokens are shared with specs that get more set value. | Keep Fury low on Defender tokens. Fury can use some tier pieces as stat upgrades, but should not beat Bear, Priest, Boomkin, Shadow, or Prot Warrior set plans. |
| `Dragonstrike` is the normal blacksmithing main-hand upgrade and should be made as soon as Nether Vortex routing allows. | Nether Vortexes for one-hand blacksmith weapons are a legitimate early physical-DPS project. Do not split Vortexes across many crafts if LC has committed to a weapon. |
| Non-blacksmith Fury has several raid weapon paths: `Talon of the Phoenix`, `Talon of Azshara`, `Rod of the Sun King`, and Orc `Netherbane`. | Fury needs an offhand lane after class-unique first claims are protected. `Talon of Azshara` stays Rogue first, `Rod` stays Enhancement first, and `Netherbane` is stronger for Orc Fury than for non-Orc Fury. |
| `Rod of the Sun King` remains attractive to Fury even if the rage proc is poor because of top-end damage. | Do not dismiss `Rod` as bad-proc trash for Fury. It is a real second-lane Fury offhand after Enhancement priority is satisfied. |
| `Tsunami Talisman` is valuable for non-DST Fury, especially progression and longer fights; `Bloodlust Brooch` remains strong when the on-use can be timed on short kills. | Keep Tsunami as DST-compensation, not a generic melee trophy. Fury should be in the non-DST lane, below Feral Cat/Arms compensation but above specs that already have better trinket plans. |
| `Serpent Spine Longbow` is wanted but Hunter BiS, so Fury expects very late priority. | Hunter first remains correct; Fury can use the bow later, or a throwing/hit option as a fallback. |

### Shaman Endgame Theorycraft

Source: Defcamp & Melderon TV, `TBC Classic Shaman Ultimate Endgame Guide`  
Transcript: [txt](sources/class-video-transcripts/defcamp-melderon-shaman-endgame.txt), [tsv](sources/class-video-transcripts/defcamp-melderon-shaman-endgame.tsv)

| Claim | LC implication |
|---|---|
| Dual-wield is the DPS model; two-handers are far behind for Enhancement DPS. | Two-handed weapons should not be routed to Enhancement for PvE DPS. |
| Two slow weapons with Windfury/Windfury scale best. A fast offhand with Flametongue can work early, but falls behind with better gear. | Protect slow one-handers for Enhancement. `Rod of the Sun King`, `Talon of the Phoenix`, and slow alternatives are not just stat sticks. |
| Weapon speed matching, sync, and stagger matter. Same-speed slow weapons are best when managed correctly; mismatched slow weapons can still be strong. | Enhancement priority should consider pair quality and speed, not only single-item EP. |
| Blacksmithing one-handers being main-hand only changed Enhancement gearing, and blacksmithing is not automatically BiS in Tier 5. | Do not make Enhancement a default first Vortex target. Their raid priority is better expressed through slow weapon drops and 4p timing. |
